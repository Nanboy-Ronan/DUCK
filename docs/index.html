Abstract

Agentic medical AI systems built on tool-using large language models are increasingly deployed for chest X-ray interpretation, yet their fairness properties remain poorly understood. Prior work evaluates end-to-end accuracy gaps across demographic groups but cannot identify where in the agentic pipeline bias originates.

We present DUCX (Decomposing Unfairness in Tool-Using Chest X-ray Agents), a framework that audits MedRAX-style agents at three complementary levels: (1) tool-exposure bias — subgroup utility gaps conditioned on whether a specific diagnostic tool was invoked; (2) tool-transition bias — subgroup differences in the tool-routing Markov chain traversed during a reasoning trajectory; and (3) LLM-reasoning bias — post-tool disparities in answer quality, hedging language, and demographic framing in the final synthesis step.

DUCX is evaluated on five driver LLMs — LLaMA3.1-8B, Ministral-3-8B, Qwen3VL-8B, Qwen3-8B, and Gemini-Flash — across two datasets: ChestAgentBench and the newly introduced MIMIC-FairnessVQA benchmark. Our decomposition reveals that bias contributions differ substantially across models and datasets, motivating targeted mitigation strategies beyond aggregate accuracy auditing.

🔬 Three Bias Lenses

DUCX decomposes agentic unfairness into three interpretable sources, each with dedicated output metrics that pin-point mitigation targets.

🛠️

Tool-Exposure Bias

When the same diagnostic tool is invoked, does its accuracy benefit differ by patient demographic? Captures disparities in tool effectiveness across groups.

conditional_tool_utility utility_gap
🔀

Tool-Transition Bias

Does the agent route male vs. female, or young vs. elderly patients through different sequences of tools? Treats trajectories as Markov chains and measures subgroup divergence.

transition_rates transition_divergence
🧠

LLM-Reasoning Bias

After tools return results, does the LLM synthesize answers equally well? Measures reasoning quality, hedging / uncertainty expression, and demographic-term frequency disparities.

judge_score hedging_gap demographic_term_gap
